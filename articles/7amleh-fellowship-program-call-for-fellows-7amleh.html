7amleh Fellowship Program - A Call to Arms Against Financial Censorship!

In a world where digital domination is the new frontier of warfare, the 7amleh Fellowship Program emerges as a beacon of hope and resistance! Launched by the Palestinian Digital Rights Coalition, this initiative is poised to tackle the unsettling realities of artificial intelligence (AI) and emerging technologies that have become tools of oppression in Palestine. With the Israeli occupation employing these digital weapons to enforce a regime of repression, the stakes couldn't be higher.

Let’s set the scene: the Israeli state has increasingly turned to tech to bolster its military strategies, deploying everything from predictive targeting systems to facial recognition technology. The result? A chilling reality where Palestinians in Gaza, the West Bank, and even Jerusalem find themselves under the watchful eye of a digital panopticon. This isn't just a local issue; the technologies refined in this conflict zone are exported globally, wreaking havoc on marginalized communities around the world. As highlighted by 7amleh, the complicity of international tech companies in this digital apartheid calls for urgent, justice-centered research.

"The absence of global enforcement and the growing convergence of military and corporate digital infrastructures demand action." - 7amleh

The 7amleh Fellowship Program is not merely an academic endeavor; it is a revolutionary response to the urgent need for critical research and advocacy at the intersection of AI, digital repression, and Palestine. Over the course of six months, this fellowship will bring together 4 to 6 researchers who are on the frontlines of digital rights, AI ethics, journalism, and conflict studies. This is a part-time, non-resident fellowship that demands a commitment of approximately 12-15 hours per week from its participants. But what’s in it for the fellows, you ask? Well, they’ll gain access to 7amleh’s rich research infrastructure, academic mentorship, and a collaborative space to produce groundbreaking knowledge that is both locally grounded and globally relevant.

But wait, there’s more! The fellows will receive monetary compensation to support their research efforts. The exact stipend remains a mystery, to be revealed in their offer letters, but it’s safe to say that this program is serious about empowering voices that are often silenced.

Throughout the fellowship, participants will navigate a rigorous timeline: from initial research design to a first draft submission by early December, and culminating in final publication by March 2025. This is not just about producing papers; it's about creating high-quality, critical research that shines a spotlight on the dark underbelly of AI and surveillance practices—especially those that originate from the Israeli context.

"This program aims to support high-quality, critical research on AI, surveillance, and digital repression as practiced in and exported from the context of Palestine." - 7amleh

The topics fellows are encouraged to explore are nothing short of explosive. From examining the implications of AI in military targeting in Gaza for international humanitarian law, to dissecting the surveillance infrastructure and predictive policing technologies in the West Bank, the scope is vast and vital. They will delve into how Israeli-developed military technologies are marketed and sold globally, and how Palestinian data—ranging from telecommunications to biometric and financial data—is captured and exploited. They will also tackle algorithmic bias and the suppression of Palestinian narratives online, shining a light on the shadowy world of coordinated disinformation campaigns targeting Palestinians and their allies.

Each fellow is expected to produce an original research paper of approximately 6,000 to 8,000 words that meets stringent academic standards. But that’s not all! They must also create a policy brief summarizing key findings and recommendations, and at least one op-ed or multimedia piece for public dissemination. This is a call to arms for researchers who are passionate about resistance and want their work to have a tangible impact.

"This fellowship will amplify Palestinian and regional voices in international digital policy spaces." - 7amleh

Moreover, active participation is a must! Monthly check-ins with fellowship managers, peer-learning sessions, and at least one public event or webinar hosted by 7amleh are all part of the program. This ensures that the knowledge produced is accessible to both Palestinian and global audiences, amplifying critical voices in the ongoing struggle against digital repression.

In conclusion, the 7amleh Fellowship Program is not just an academic initiative; it’s a powerful movement aiming to confront the financial censorship that underpins the digital repression faced by Palestinians. As the world becomes increasingly digitized, the need for justice-centered research and advocacy has never been more urgent. Will you answer the call? The time to act is now!
