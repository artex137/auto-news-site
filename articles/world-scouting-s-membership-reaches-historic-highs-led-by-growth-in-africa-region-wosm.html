World Scouting’s membership reaches historic highs, led by growth in Africa region - WOSM

Scouting on Steroids: Membership Hits Jaw-Dropping 60 Million by 2025!

In a shocking twist that has the world buzzing, the World Organization of the Scout Movement (WOSM) has announced that its membership is on the verge of reaching a staggering 60 million by 2025! Yes, you heard that right—60 million! This monumental surge is not just a fluke; it reflects a deliberate expansion into new communities, an explosion of volunteer networks, and a deepening of partnerships that would make even the most seasoned politicians envious.

“With a reach of 60 million young people and volunteers, Scouts have incredible potential to change the world,” declared David Berg, Secretary General of World Scouting, in a statement that could easily serve as a rallying cry for a new movement.

The organization's latest census figures reveal a registered membership of over 51.4 million in 2023, as National Scout Organizations (NSOs) rebound from the pandemic-induced slump. But hold on—this isn't just about numbers; it's about a global phenomenon that’s transforming lives and communities.

Africa: The New Powerhouse of Scouting

Now, let’s dive into the specifics. The Africa Scout region is leading the charge, with membership figures skyrocketing by an astonishing 95.9%! That’s right, nearly doubling to almost 10 million! Two nations are making headlines: Nigeria and Kenya. Nigeria added a whopping 2.3 million new members, while Kenya welcomed 2.2 million. These growth rates catapult Nigeria and Kenya into the second- and third-largest NSOs in the Movement, leaving other regions in the dust.

“We are witnessing a scouting revolution in Africa,” said a source close to the movement, who requested anonymity due to the sensitive nature of the information.

Could this be the dawn of a new era in youth engagement on the continent? Only time will tell, but the numbers speak for themselves.

Asia-Pacific: Riding the Growth Wave

But wait, there’s more! The Asia-Pacific Region is also riding high, with a rise to 37.2 million registered young people and volunteers—a 4.8% increase year-on-year. The Philippines alone registered over 772,000 new members, while India saw an increase of more than 672,000, thanks to the National Growth Strategy initiated in 2020.

“The Bharat Scouts and Guides has experienced a significant leap in membership growth,” stated Madhusudan Avala, Additional International Commissioner of India’s The Bharat Scouts and Guides.

He revealed ambitious plans to reach 4.5 million Scouts by 2025 and a jaw-dropping target of 10 million by 2028! Are we witnessing the birth of a scouting empire in South Asia?

Europe and Beyond: Mixed Results

Empowering Women: A Milestone Year

2023 is also a landmark year for gender inclusion in Scouting, with an unprecedented 18.8 million girls and women registered. The Asia-Pacific Region leads the charge with over 13.5 million, followed by Africa (3.9 million) and Europe (646,000). This surge is not merely a statistic; it reflects targeted efforts by NSOs to create inclusive and empowering programs that resonate with young women.

“We are committed to building a more inclusive movement,” a spokesperson for WOSM emphasized, echoing the sentiments of many NSOs.

Training and initiatives aimed at supporting girls' leadership have played a crucial role in this phenomenal increase. Is this the beginning of a new wave of female leaders in scouting?

Rebuilding After COVID: A Collective Effort

As the world slowly emerges from the shadow of the COVID-19 pandemic, NSOs have been working tirelessly to rebuild and revitalize their memberships. Long-term growth strategies, expanded resources, and innovative programs are now the norm rather than the exception. A staggering 134 out of 230 NSOs submitted data for the latest census, painting a vivid picture of the global scouting landscape.

So, what does all this mean? It means that scouting is not just surviving; it is thriving! As we look ahead to 2025 and beyond, the world should brace itself for a scouting movement that promises to be bigger, bolder, and more inclusive than ever before. Will this be a game-changer for youth engagement globally? Only time will tell, but one thing is for sure: Scouting is back, and it’s here to stay!
