The 44 Most Powerful Players in Podcasting in 2025: A Look at Joe Rogan's Dominance

As we dive headfirst into 2025, the podcasting landscape is akin to a wild west showdown, with a handful of influential figures vying for the top spot. Among them, the indomitable Joe Rogan continues to reign supreme, proving that his podcast is not just a passing fad but a cultural phenomenon. With millions of loyal listeners hanging on his every word, Rogan's influence is undeniable—and so are the controversies swirling around him.

The Hollywood Reporter has rolled out its annual list of the most powerful players in podcasting, and it’s no surprise that Rogan is featured prominently. His podcast, "The Joe Rogan Experience," has consistently topped charts and shattered records, solidifying his status as the go-to voice for millions. But what exactly is fueling this juggernaut? According to the report, Rogan's blend of celebrity interviews and unfiltered conversations resonates with an audience hungry for authenticity in an age of manufactured content.

“Podcasts are adulting, in other words,” writes THR’s James Hibberd in his accompanying analysis, “which signals the medium’s maturity and increasing dominance.”

While Rogan's podcast has been a beacon of success, it hasn't been without its fair share of controversies. Critics argue that his platform has become a breeding ground for misinformation, with some questioning the integrity of his guests and the topics discussed. In a world grappling with "fake news," Rogan's approach has sparked heated debates about the responsibility of podcasters as influencers.

In the latest report, it’s revealed that Rogan's show has pulled in staggering numbers, boasting an audience that dwarfs many traditional media outlets. With over 11 million downloads per episode, his reach is extensive, effectively making him a media mogul in his own right. This raises the question: does Rogan wield too much power? As the podcasting sphere evolves, is it time for accountability?

In stark contrast to Rogan's unfiltered style, other podcasters are also making waves. For instance, Alex Cooper of "Call Her Daddy" has made a name for herself with her bold commentary and strategic moves, including a jaw-dropping $125 million deal with SiriusXM. Her ability to engage listeners on topics ranging from pop culture to politics has positioned her as a formidable competitor to Rogan, even occasionally surpassing him in the charts. Cooper's rise exemplifies the shifting dynamics in the podcast world, with an emphasis on personality-driven content.

“Like classic talk shows, I think live content and audience interactivity will play key roles in the format’s growth,” Cooper stated, hinting at the future direction of podcasting.

Meanwhile, others like the duo behind "Stuff You Should Know" continue to capture audiences with their eclectic mix of topics. With over 1,500 episodes under their belt, they have mastered the art of making trivia engaging and entertaining. However, they express skepticism about the trend towards video podcasting, arguing that the essence of podcasting lies in its audio format. This sentiment resonates with many purists who fear the dilution of the medium's authenticity.

But let’s not forget Joe Budden, who is shaking up the hip-hop podcasting scene with his namesake show. Budden has revealed that his podcast network is projected to generate over $20 million in 2025, highlighting the lucrative potential of podcasting for those who can tap into the right audience. His candid discussions and industry insights have made him a key player, proving that hip-hop and podcasting are a match made in media heaven.

On the flip side, the likes of Tucker Carlson are carving out a niche in the conservative podcasting landscape. Carlson’s show has drawn attention for its provocative takes, including a viral moment where he confronted Senator Ted Cruz about his lack of knowledge on Iran's population. This incident not only showcased Carlson’s confrontational style but also reflected the growing polarization within the podcasting world.

“It’s us versus algorithms,” Budden lamented, highlighting the challenges podcasters face in navigating an increasingly digital landscape.

As the podcasting landscape continues to evolve, the question remains: will Joe Rogan maintain his throne, or will emerging talents like Cooper and Budden topple him from his perch? With the medium growing more competitive and diverse, it’s clear that the future of podcasting will be shaped by those who can adapt and innovate.

As we look ahead, one thing is certain: the podcasting industry is far from stagnant. With new voices rising and established ones facing scrutiny, the battle for the airwaves is just heating up. Will we see more accountability in the realm of podcasting, or will the trend of unchecked influence continue? Only time will tell, but for now, the stage is set for an electrifying showdown in the world of audio storytelling.
