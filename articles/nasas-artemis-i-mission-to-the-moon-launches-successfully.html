NASA's Artemis I Mission to the Moon Launches Successfully

2025-08-13 06:30 UTC

Artemis I Launches from Kennedy Space Center

NASA's Artemis I mission successfully launched on November 16, 2021, from Kennedy Space Center in Florida. The mission aims to return humans to the Moon and establish a sustainable presence there by the end of the decade.

The Space Launch System (SLS) rocket, carrying the Orion spacecraft, lifted off at 1:47 a.m. EST. This marks the first integrated flight of the SLS and Orion, paving the way for future crewed missions.

"Today, we are all part of a great adventure that will take us back to the Moon and beyond," said NASA Administrator Bill Nelson.

The Artemis program is designed to explore the Moon and prepare for future missions to Mars. The Orion spacecraft will orbit the Moon before returning to Earth, testing critical systems for future crewed flights.

NASA plans to use the data gathered during Artemis I to inform the next missions, which will include astronauts aboard the Orion spacecraft.
