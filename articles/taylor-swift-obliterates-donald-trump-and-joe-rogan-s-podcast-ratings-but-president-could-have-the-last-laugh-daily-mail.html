Taylor Swift Obliterates Donald Trump and Joe Rogan's Podcast Ratings... But President Could Have the Last Laugh

In a twist that could only be scripted in Hollywood, Taylor Swift has decisively trounced Donald Trump and Joe Rogan's podcast ratings, but don’t count the former president out just yet! On August 14, 2025, the world witnessed a seismic shift in podcasting dynamics as the newest episode of the 'New Heights' podcast, featuring pop sensation Taylor Swift, shattered records and left Trump's previous podcast appearance in the dust.

Swift's cameo on the podcast, co-hosted by Kansas City Chiefs superstar Travis Kelce, was highly anticipated, especially with buzz surrounding the unveiling of her upcoming album, 'The Life of a Showgirl.' Fans—dubbed Swifties—didn’t just tune in; they crashed YouTube! The episode amassed a staggering 1.3 million live viewers, obliterating the previous record held by none other than Joe Rogan, whose podcast episode featuring Trump garnered 26 million views within 24 hours. It’s a classic case of “you can’t beat the original,” but Swift’s feat is nothing short of remarkable.

“Shoutout all the 92%ers and Swifties for actually helping us break the internet,” the New Heights X account triumphantly declared post-stream.

But let’s dissect the numbers. While Swift’s live stream was historic, it still has a mountain to climb to reach Rogan's 24-hour viewer mark. As of the latest tally, Swift's episode has racked up 9.5 million views in just 14 hours, with a whopping 6 million of those coming in the first five hours. Yes, it’s impressive, but will it ever come close to surpassing Rogan’s juggernaut? Only time will tell.

Swift's foray into podcasting comes with its own set of political undertones. After all, this isn’t just about the numbers; it’s about the narrative. Swift, known for her strong political stance, endorsed Democrat nominee Kamala Harris ahead of the 2024 election, drawing ire from Trump, who infamously quipped that Swift was “no longer hot” after her endorsement. The tension between the pop star and the former president could not be more palpable, adding layers to this already explosive story.

But let’s not overlook the broader implications. This podcast episode didn’t merely eclipse Trump’s ratings; it signaled a cultural shift. Swift’s influence extends far beyond music; she’s a pop culture powerhouse who can mobilize millions in mere moments. The surge in subscriptions for the New Heights podcast, which jumped from 2.85 million to 2.97 million following the episode, underscores her magnetic pull. Clearly, the Swift effect is real, and it’s reshaping the podcast landscape.

“1.3 million views is the epitome of numerology,” tweeted Jason Kelce, referencing Swift’s affinity for the number 13.

However, let’s not rush to declare Swift the undefeated champion. Rogan’s podcasting empire remains formidable. His episode with Trump, released during the height of the 2024 election cycle, was strategically timed and leveraged the political climate to its full advantage. With 26 million views, it’s a juggernaut that continues to loom large over the podcasting world.

And what about the future? Will Swift’s new album, anticipated to drop soon, bring in even more listeners for the New Heights podcast? Will Rogan retaliate with a star-studded lineup of his own? The stakes are high, and the battle for podcast supremacy is anything but over.

In the realm of celebrity and politics, the lines are increasingly blurred. Swift’s engagement with the podcasting world exemplifies a new era where artists wield considerable influence over public opinion and media consumption. With every episode, the stakes rise, and the audience becomes ever more polarized.

As we watch this saga unfold, one thing is for certain: the clash between Taylor Swift and Donald Trump, manifesting in the podcasting arena, is a spectacle that will continue to capture public attention. Will Swift’s moment in the spotlight translate into lasting influence? Or will Trump’s established podcasting dominance remain unchallenged? Only time will reveal the outcome of this high-stakes game.

For now, Swift reigns supreme in the podcast ratings, but don’t underestimate the resilience and strategic prowess of Trump and Rogan. The podcasting landscape is as volatile as ever, and the battle for the hearts and ears of listeners is far from over.
