Federal Prosecutor Mike Gordon is No Deep State Operative

In a shocking turn of events that has left many scratching their heads, U.S. Attorney General Pam Bondi, a name synonymous with controversy, has fired Assistant U.S. Attorney Michael Gordon, and the implications are as twisted as a political thriller. This dramatic dismissal, executed “without cause,” follows Gordon’s stellar performance in the courtroom, raising eyebrows and questions about the integrity of the justice system in the era of Trump.

Gordon’s résumé is nothing short of impressive. He has successfully prosecuted significant cases, including that of Leo Govoni, a multi-million dollar fraudster accused of pocketing funds meant for special needs children. But Bondi’s decision to give Gordon the boot—alongside two other prosecutors who bravely tackled the January 6th rioters—smacks of political maneuvering that many have come to associate with the dark underbelly of what some are calling the “Deep State.”

“I was tasked to do justice without fear or favor,” Gordon stated, vehemently denying any affiliation with the so-called Deep State. “I am not a political operative; I am a public servant.”

Gordon’s dismissal is as perplexing as it is alarming. He claims that his firing was a blatant violation of the Federal Civil Service Act, as he was let go without due process. It’s a shocking revelation that casts a long shadow over the U.S. Department of Justice, raising concerns about the erosion of civil service protections that were painstakingly put in place after the Nixon scandals. Gordon’s worries are not unfounded; he fears that without these legal guardrails, we could witness a dystopian future where unqualified individuals are appointed to power based solely on their political connections.

Let’s rewind the clock to January 6, 2021, a day that will forever be etched in American history. As the Capitol was stormed, Gordon, like many Americans, watched in horror. However, unlike most, he stepped up to the plate when the District of Columbia federal prosecutor called for volunteers to help prosecute the insurrectionists. Gordon didn’t hide behind a desk; he worked remotely from Tampa, preparing cases during the pandemic before flying to D.C. for court appearances. His commitment led to numerous convictions in what has been described as the largest mass prosecution effort in a single federal court in U.S. history.

“I was there to uphold the law and protect democracy,” Gordon said, reflecting on his dedication to justice amidst chaos.

But here’s where it gets even juicier: just as Gordon’s last trial was deliberating, news broke that Trump had pardoned ALL January 6th defendants. Imagine the frustration! Gordon returned to Tampa to continue his fight against fraud, only to be blindsided by a firing letter from Bondi on June 27, 2023. “I was handed a letter from an administrative assistant, telling me I was fired effective immediately, with no cause given,” he lamented. This was not just an affront to Gordon but a slap in the face to the entire U.S. Attorney’s Office in Tampa, which was reportedly outraged by the decision.

In an extraordinary display of dissent, the morale at the office plummeted. When Bondi visited the Tampa office after the firing, staff members were so incensed that they boycotted a photo-op with her. “People were asked to participate, but most of the office refused to take a photograph with her,” Gordon revealed, highlighting the widespread discontent that followed his dismissal.

“It’s a nightmare scenario for the country,” Gordon warned, “where unqualified people are put in positions of power just because they are friends with the right person.”

As the dust settles, Gordon is not sitting idly by. He is currently perfecting his chocolate chip cookie recipe and spending quality time with his children, but he’s also preparing to take legal action against the U.S. government. “I’m confident that I will find a new job because I am no Deep State operative,” he asserted, standing firm in his belief that justice must prevail.

This saga raises critical questions about the integrity of our justice system and the lengths to which political actors will go to silence those who stand for justice. Will Michael Gordon’s story be a cautionary tale for future public servants, or will it ignite a movement to protect the very foundations of our democracy? Only time will tell, but one thing is certain: the battle for justice in America is far from over.

As we watch this story unfold, let’s remain vigilant and demand accountability from those in power. The fate of our democracy may very well depend on it.
