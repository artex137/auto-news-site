Discover this week's must-read trade stories - The World Economic Forum

Hold onto your hats! The world of trade is in a frenzy, and the headlines are anything but dull. As of August 7, 2023, the United States has unleashed a tidal wave of tariffs on over 60 nations, marking the beginning of a new global trade order that has major partners like the EU and India in an uproar. What does this mean for the average consumer? According to the Yale Budget Lab, brace yourselves for a staggering $2,400 hit to your wallets annually! Yes, you read that right—tariffs are back, and they're more vicious than ever.

“The highest average US tariff rate since 1933 is now in effect,” reports the Yale Budget Lab. “This will disproportionately affect lower-income households who spend a greater share of their income on imports.”

The chaos began on a seemingly ordinary day that would soon be etched in the annals of economic turmoil. On July 3, the US slapped a 20% tariff on Vietnam, quickly escalating to 40% on trans-shipments by July 6. But the real shocker came on July 7 when tariffs ranging from 25% to 40% were imposed on 14 nations. Fast forward to August 1, and we saw a jaw-dropping 10% to 41% tariff increase on 69 nations. Can you say “trade war”? The impacts are already sending ripples through global markets, leading to a rollercoaster of stock prices and gold futures breaking records before plummeting due to uncertainty over Swiss gold bars.

But wait, there’s more! In a shocking twist, President Trump has hinted at a potential 100% tariff on imported computer chips. Yes, you heard that right—100%! This madcap move is accompanied by a deal involving chipmakers Nvidia and AMD, where they agreed to pay the US government 15% of their revenue from certain AI chip sales to China. This is not just a tariff; it's a high-stakes game of poker where the chips are literally chips!

“The tariff dispute between the US and India has escalated, impacting the strategic partnership between the two countries,” warns the White House, as they double the tariff on India to 50% due to its continued purchases of Russian oil.

The fallout from these tariffs isn’t just a theoretical exercise. In Canada, the manufacturing sector has been hit hard, losing tens of thousands of jobs in July alone, with the Bank of Canada reporting a significant drop in hiring intentions among Canadian companies. Meanwhile, the EU is feeling the heat as a new analysis reveals that 5.2 million jobs in the region are at risk due to these aggressive US tariffs.

And what about the World Trade Organization (WTO)? They’ve slashed their 2026 global merchandise trade growth forecast from 2.5% to a mere 1.8%. Their economists are sounding the alarm bells, warning that the full impact of these recent tariff hikes will be felt next year, leading to a dramatic drop in business confidence and investment.

“Uncertainty remains one of the most disruptive forces in the global trading environment,” the WTO cautions, and they’re not wrong!

Let’s take a moment to dissect the absurdity of it all. On one hand, we have the US government throwing tariffs around like confetti, and on the other, countries like Mexico are granted a 90-day reprieve to continue negotiations. As the world watches in disbelief, it seems like a game of international chess where the stakes are nothing less than the global economy itself.

And while all of this is unfolding, the World Economic Forum (WEF) is busy trying to facilitate trade in developing countries through the Global Alliance for Trade Facilitation. This initiative aims to help governments implement the WTO’s Trade Facilitation Agreement, cutting the average rate of physical inspections of food and beverages by an impressive 30%. But will these efforts be enough to counteract the chaos unleashed by the US tariffs?

“The Alliance delivered $8.8 million in savings for importers in the first 18 months of operation,” heralds the WEF, but one has to wonder—can it truly offset the economic devastation caused by tariffs?

As we stand on the precipice of a new trade order, the question remains: will these tariffs usher in an era of economic prosperity, or will they plunge us into a deeper abyss? The global trading landscape is shifting beneath our feet, and the stakes have never been higher. Buckle up, folks; it’s going to be a wild ride!
