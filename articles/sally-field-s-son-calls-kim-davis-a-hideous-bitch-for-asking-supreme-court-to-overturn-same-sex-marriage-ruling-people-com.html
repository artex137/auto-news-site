Sally Field's Son Slams Kim Davis in Explosive Social Media Outburst

In a whirlwind of social media drama, Sally Field's son, Sam Greisman, has unleashed a torrent of invective against the infamous Kim Davis, the former Kentucky county clerk who became a household name for her staunch opposition to same-sex marriage. Greisman, 37, didn’t hold back, branding Davis a “hideous bitch” in a recent post on X (formerly Twitter), igniting a firestorm of commentary and debate as Davis attempts to resurrect her controversial legacy.

Kim Davis, 59, has filed a request with the U.S. Supreme Court to overturn its historic 2015 ruling in Obergefell v. Hodges, which granted same-sex couples the constitutional right to marry. This landmark decision was the culmination of legal battles involving 16 same-sex couple plaintiffs from Michigan, Ohio, Kentucky, and Tennessee, and led to the legalization of gay marriage across all 50 states. Yet, Davis has not faded quietly into the background; her recent legal maneuvering has thrust her back into the spotlight, much to the chagrin of advocates for LGBTQ+ rights.

Back in 2015, Davis gained notoriety when she refused to issue marriage licenses to same-sex couples, citing religious exemptions based on the First Amendment of the Constitution. This defiance led to a barrage of lawsuits, including the notable Miller v. Davis case, which she took all the way to the Supreme Court. When her appeal was denied, Davis continued her refusal and was ultimately thrown behind bars for contempt of court, spending nearly a week incarcerated while her deputies issued licenses in her absence.

“Not this hideous bitch again,” Greisman exclaimed, responding to headlines about Davis's latest legal antics.

In the aftermath of her release, Davis faced a political reckoning, losing her reelection bid to a Democrat in 2018. The legal consequences of her actions continue to haunt her; in 2023, a jury awarded a gay couple, David Ermold and David Moore, $100,000 due to her refusal to issue marriage licenses. Just a year later, a federal judge ordered Davis to pay an additional $260,000 in legal fees to those who sought licenses from her office, underscoring the financial fallout of her defiance.

The public's reaction to Davis's latest legal gambit has been swift and scathing. Notably, even prominent figures from the 2016 presidential election weighed in. Hillary Clinton took to X to declare, “Marriage equality is the law of the land. Officials should be held to their duty to uphold the law—end of story.” Meanwhile, Donald Trump, who had previously commented on her incarceration, stated, “The decision’s been made, and that is the law of the land.”

Greisman’s scathing remarks aren't merely personal; they tap into a broader narrative of hypocrisy. Critics have pointed out the irony of Davis, who has been married four times, championing the sanctity of marriage while simultaneously undermining the legal rights of same-sex couples. The Wisconsin-based non-profit Freedom From Religion Foundation chimed in, quipping that divorce rates are lower among same-sex couples than heterosexual ones, adding, “But, Kim Davis’s four marriages are really helping to skew the numbers in their favor.”

Sally Field, the celebrated actress and activist, has long been a vocal supporter of LGBTQ+ rights. At 78, she has shared her life with her two elder sons, Peter and Eli, from her first marriage, and Sam with her second husband, Alan Greisman. Field has expressed her commitment to advocacy, stating, “I’m fighting for the future of my son,” in reference to her and Sam’s activism surrounding the Equality Act, which aims to protect LGBTQ+ individuals from discrimination.

As Davis continues her legal battles, the public remains divided. Supporters of Davis argue for religious freedoms, while opponents, including Greisman and his mother, advocate for equality and the rights of all individuals to love freely. The clash of ideologies showcases a nation still grappling with the complexities of marriage, religion, and civil rights.

In an era where social media can amplify voices, Greisman’s outburst serves as a rallying cry for those who believe that the fight for equality is far from over. As Kim Davis attempts to rewrite history with her latest Supreme Court filing, it’s clear that the battle lines are drawn, and the discourse surrounding same-sex marriage and religious liberty will continue to ignite passionate responses from both sides.
