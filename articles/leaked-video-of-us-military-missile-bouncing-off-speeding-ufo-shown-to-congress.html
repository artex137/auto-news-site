Leaked video of US military missile ‘bouncing’ off speeding UFO shown to Congress

On 9 September 2024, a House Oversight subcommittee hearing revealed leaked footage showing a US military Hellfire missile appearing to ‘bounce’ off a shiny object off the coast of Yemen on 30 October 2024. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with the incident.

This video, presented by Rep. Eric Burlison of Missouri, has raised significant interest regarding Unidentified Anomalous Phenomena (UAPs), the military's term for UFOs. During the hearing, military whistleblowers testified under oath about their encounters with UFOs and accused the government of covering up information related to these phenomena.

Key facts

  • Event date: 30 October 2024
  • Hearing date: 9 September 2024
  • Location: Off the coast of Yemen
  • Video source: Anonymous whistleblower
  • Presented by: Rep. Eric Burlison (Missouri)
  • Witness testimony: Former Air Force military police officer Nuccetelli

Timeline

On 30 October 2024, a US military Hellfire missile was fired at a shiny object off the coast of Yemen. The incident was captured on video, which has since been shared with Congress.

On 9 September 2024, the video was presented during a House Oversight subcommittee hearing focusing on UAPs. Rep. Eric Burlison revealed the footage, prompting discussions about the military's findings on UFOs.

Official statements

“The video provides exceptional evidence of UFOs,” said former Air Force military police officer Nuccetelli during the hearing.

Context

This incident is part of a broader discussion on UAPs, which have gained increased attention from lawmakers and the public in recent years. Military whistleblowers have previously raised concerns about the government's transparency regarding UFO sightings and encounters.

What’s next

Congress may continue to investigate the implications of the video and the testimonies provided during the hearing, as well as the military's handling of UAP information. Further hearings and inquiries are expected as lawmakers seek clarity on the issue.
