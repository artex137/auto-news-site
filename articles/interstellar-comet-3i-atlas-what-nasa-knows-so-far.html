Interstellar Comet 3I⧸ATLAS - What NASA Knows So Far

NASA has confirmed the presence of interstellar comet 3I⧸ATLAS, which was first detected in the United States in 2019. This comet is notable for being the second known interstellar object to pass through our solar system.

The discovery of 3I⧸ATLAS is significant as it provides insights into the composition and behavior of objects from outside our solar system. NASA is currently monitoring the comet's trajectory and characteristics as it approaches the inner solar system.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I⧸ATLAS was detected in 2019.
  • It is the second known interstellar object identified by scientists.
  • NASA is actively tracking its trajectory.
  • Location of detection: United States.
  • Current status: Approaching the inner solar system.

Timeline

In 2019, astronomers first detected interstellar comet 3I⧸ATLAS as it entered the solar system. Since then, NASA has been observing its path and analyzing its composition.

As of now, the comet is on a trajectory that will bring it closer to the sun, allowing for further study of its features.

Official statements

“The discovery of 3I⧸ATLAS opens new avenues for understanding the nature of interstellar objects,” said a NASA spokesperson.

Context

3I⧸ATLAS follows the first known interstellar object, ‘Oumuamua, which was detected in 2017. Both comets have sparked interest in the scientific community regarding their origins and implications for our understanding of the universe.

What’s next

NASA will continue to monitor 3I⧸ATLAS as it approaches the sun, with upcoming observations scheduled to gather more data on its characteristics and behavior.
