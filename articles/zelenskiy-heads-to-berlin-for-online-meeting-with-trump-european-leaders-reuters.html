Zelenskiy heads to Berlin for online meeting with Trump, European leaders - Reuters

August 13, 2025

Berlin, Germany – Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskiy is set to participate in an online meeting with former U.S. President Donald Trump and several European leaders, according to reports from Reuters. The meeting, scheduled for later this week, aims to address ongoing geopolitical tensions in Eastern Europe, particularly in light of Russia's actions in Ukraine.

The virtual summit comes at a time when Ukraine continues to grapple with the ramifications of the ongoing conflict with Russia, which has escalated since the invasion in February 2022. Zelenskiy's participation signals a continued effort to garner international support and solidarity against what many in the West view as Russian aggression.

Trump, who has been a polarizing figure in American and international politics, has previously expressed a willingness to engage with global leaders on issues of security and diplomacy. However, his approach to Ukraine has raised eyebrows. During his presidency, Trump was impeached for allegedly pressuring Zelenskiy to investigate his political rival, Joe Biden, which has cast a long shadow over U.S.-Ukraine relations.

Despite the controversy surrounding Trump, Zelenskiy's decision to engage with him reflects a pragmatic approach to diplomacy. Analysts suggest that the Ukrainian leader may be seeking to leverage Trump's influence among Republican lawmakers, many of whom have expressed skepticism about continued U.S. support for Ukraine.

According to a source familiar with the meeting's agenda, topics will likely include military aid, economic assistance, and strategies for countering Russian disinformation campaigns. The involvement of European leaders, whose countries have been significantly affected by the conflict, underscores the importance of a united front in addressing the crisis.

Experts have noted that while Trump's past comments on Ukraine have been controversial, his administration did approve military aid to Ukraine, which included lethal aid that was previously withheld. This duality in Trump's approach complicates the narrative surrounding his potential influence in the ongoing conflict.

“Zelenskiy is in a tough position,” said Dr. Maria Ivanova, a political analyst specializing in Eastern European affairs. “He needs to maintain support from a wide array of political figures, including those who may not have been supportive of Ukraine in the past. Engaging with Trump could be a strategic move to ensure that support continues.”

However, skepticism remains about the effectiveness of such meetings. Critics argue that Trump's unpredictable nature and his past foreign policy decisions could undermine the stability of U.S. support for Ukraine. “There is a significant risk that Trump’s involvement could lead to mixed messages,” said Dr. Ivanova. “It is crucial for Zelenskiy to navigate this carefully.”

The meeting is also significant in the context of Europe’s evolving stance on defense and security. European leaders have increasingly recognized the need for a cohesive strategy to counter Russian influence, particularly as energy prices soar and public sentiment shifts regarding military support for Ukraine. The outcomes of this meeting could have far-reaching implications for both U.S. and European policies regarding the conflict.

In recent months, European nations have ramped up military assistance to Ukraine, with Germany and France leading efforts to provide advanced weaponry. The online meeting could serve as a platform for Zelenskiy to reinforce the necessity of continued support from both sides of the Atlantic.

As the meeting approaches, speculation is rife about what Trump’s participation will mean for the dynamics of U.S.-Ukraine relations. Some analysts suggest that Trump’s return to the political stage, should he decide to run for president again in 2024, could complicate matters further. “If Trump were to regain the presidency, his approach to Ukraine could shift dramatically, depending on the political landscape at that time,” noted Dr. Ivanova.

Ultimately, the meeting between Zelenskiy, Trump, and European leaders represents a critical juncture in the ongoing conflict. As Ukraine continues to face existential threats, the need for a unified and strategic response has never been more pressing. The outcomes of this virtual summit could influence not only the immediate future of Ukraine but also the broader geopolitical landscape in Europe.

As the world watches, the implications of this meeting will likely resonate beyond the confines of the virtual room, shaping the discourse on international support for Ukraine and the West's approach to Russia in the months to come.
