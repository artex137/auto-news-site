Meet a PA Mayor Who Embraces UFOs and Small Town Quirkiness

In a world where political figures often play it safe, one mayor is boldly going where few would dare: into the realm of UFOs and small-town eccentricity. Meet Michele Bannon, the mayor of Carbondale, Pennsylvania, a city that proudly boasts its quirky charm and a history steeped in the extraordinary. With a population of around 8,800 and a location just 19 miles northeast of Scranton, Carbondale has become a beacon of both community spirit and otherworldly intrigue.

Bannon, who has lived in Carbondale her entire life, exudes a contagious enthusiasm for her hometown. “We’re very unfiltered,” she told PA Local, reflecting on the town’s character. “We do a lot of quirky things. We have fun events.” One of the most significant events in Carbondale’s history is the infamous UFO sighting of November 9, 1974, an incident that has transcended time and continues to captivate the imagination of locals and visitors alike.

“I’m one of the believers that something was there. I don't know what it was.” - Michele Bannon

The 1974 UFO sighting has become a cornerstone of local lore, celebrated annually with a fall festival that draws crowds eager to experience the bizarre and the unexplained. Bannon recalls the day vividly, stating, “I lived down the street from where it happened. In fact, WVIA just did a 15-minute documentary, and it’s absolutely fantastic.” At the tender age of five, she was present during the chaos that unfolded when locals flocked to the park to witness what they believed was an extraterrestrial encounter. “There were so many people there you couldn’t get close to it,” she reminisced.

But what exactly did the residents of Carbondale witness that fateful day? Bannon remains skeptical of the explanations that have emerged over the years, firmly declaring, “It was not a lantern. That was a hoax.” She stands by the testimonies of those who were present, asserting, “They gave all justified reasons” for what they saw. The local lore has evolved into a celebration of the unknown, as the city embraces its reputation as a hub for UFO enthusiasts.

In a delightful twist of fate, Carbondale's emergency call numbers are ironically linked to the UFO phenomenon, as Bannon gleefully points out, “We’re area 51. Isn’t that ironically funny?” Although the emergency response operates under the standard 911 system, local radio communications refer to units as “51 fire” and “51 police,” adding a layer of whimsicality to the town’s identity.

But the quirkiness doesn’t end there. Bannon has spearheaded a mural project that showcases the town’s character and celebrates its unique history. “A couple years ago, we did a program called Heart & Soul, where you go out and interview your people,” she explained. The resulting murals reflect the community's desires and passions, with one particularly eye-catching piece featuring an alien adorned with a fire hat and the number 51. “I embrace the quirky. We’re very quirky, by the way. I embrace it because I love it,” Bannon declared with pride.

For those who think small towns lack amenities, Bannon is quick to dispel that notion. “We have a fully functioning city. We have a full-time police and fire department. We have our own library, we have our own YMCA,” she stated, emphasizing the abundance of resources available to residents and visitors alike. Carbondale also boasts a vibrant food scene, with residents proudly identifying as foodies and celebrating the local culinary offerings.

As Bannon reflects on her journey in public service—spanning nearly three decades in various roles, including city clerk before her election as mayor in 2023—she acknowledges the responsibility she feels to lead her community. “It was my responsibility to step forward and lead,” she asserted, highlighting her deep-rooted connection to the town. “I realized that by working here for so long and being so in tune... it would be the best thing for myself and the city to join those two together.”

“We have a lot of beautiful parks. We have a lot of cool little shops.” - Michele Bannon

So, what’s next for Carbondale under Bannon’s leadership? The mayor envisions a future that embraces both the town’s rich history and its quirky character. “We have a lot of great restaurants in town. We’re a bunch of foodies up here,” she enthused, alluding to the vibrant community spirit that defines Carbondale.

As the UFO festival approaches, residents and visitors alike will gather to celebrate the town’s unique blend of charm, intrigue, and quirkiness. In a world that often feels increasingly homogenized, Carbondale stands as a testament to the power of embracing the unconventional. As Bannon aptly puts it, “I love that.”
