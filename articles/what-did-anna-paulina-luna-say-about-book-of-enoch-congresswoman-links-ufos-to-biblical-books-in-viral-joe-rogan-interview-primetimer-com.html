What did Anna Paulina Luna say about Book of Enoch? Congresswoman links UFOs to biblical books in viral Joe Rogan interview

In a jaw-dropping episode of The Joe Rogan Experience that has sent shockwaves through both the political and paranormal communities, U.S. Representative Anna Paulina Luna (R-FL) has made some eyebrow-raising claims about unidentified aerial phenomena (UAPs) and their connections to ancient biblical texts, specifically the enigmatic Book of Enoch. Yes, you heard that right! An elected official is boldly suggesting that UFOs and ancient angels might be interlinked in ways that could make your head spin.

During her January 31, 2023, appearance on the podcast, Luna, who chairs the House Task Force on the Declassification of Federal Secrets, revealed that she has reviewed classified materials related to UAPs and even claims to have seen photographic evidence of vehicles that are “not made by mankind.” And just in case you thought she was pulling your leg, she emphasized that the technology depicted in these images is “beyond our current capabilities.” Imagine a caveman trying to comprehend a smartphone—that’s the analogy she used.

“They call them interdimensional beings...They’re able to move in ways that don’t make sense with our current understanding of physics… moving outside of time and space,”

she told Rogan, sending ripples of intrigue through the audience. But wait, there’s more! Luna went on to assert that these “interdimensional beings” could be linked to the “missing books of the Bible,” particularly the Book of Enoch. According to her, the ancient text provides historical context for these modern-day phenomena.

The Book of Enoch, a text that has long been relegated to the fringes of biblical scholarship, is classified as pseudepigrapha—works attributed to biblical figures but written long after their deaths. While it remains a part of the Ethiopian Orthodox biblical canon, most Christian denominations do not recognize it as scripture. Critics have long questioned its authorship, prompting debates about its relevance and authenticity.

Luna’s claims delve into the Book of Enoch’s narratives, particularly the “fall of angels” and their alleged interactions with humans. She suggested that these celestial beings imparted advanced knowledge to early humans, covering topics like astronomy and metalworking, thus playing a pivotal role in the development of civilization. This, she argues, led to the creation of what she terms a “hybrid race.”

“So, if you read it, it talks about the fall of angels. Thus creating really the precursor of civilization that led to the first flood,”

Luna elaborated. The congresswoman paints the Book of Enoch as one of the oldest sources of mythological narratives, asserting, “This is kind of the OG text that leads to those stories.” In her view, this ancient literature could hold the key to understanding not only our past but also the mysteries of the universe.

But hold on a second! Is Anna Paulina Luna simply a voice of reason in a sea of governmental secrecy, or is she peddling conspiracy theories that could undermine public trust? She accused defense contractors and unelected officials of withholding UAP-related materials, even from other members of Congress. “This type of secret being withheld has the potential to reduce public trust, especially if that technology is of non-human origin,” she warned.

As a congresswoman delving into the realms of ancient texts and extraterrestrial beings, Luna’s remarks are bound to raise eyebrows, ignite debates, and perhaps even fuel conspiracy theories. Are we on the brink of a new understanding of human history, or is this merely the latest episode in a long-running saga of political sensationalism?

While the implications of her claims are staggering, it’s imperative to scrutinize the sources and evidence supporting her assertions. The Book of Enoch may have been a hot topic in antiquity, but its relevance in contemporary discussions about UFOs and interdimensional beings remains dubious at best. Critics may argue that Luna is leveraging sensational narratives to garner attention and support, casting a shadow over the serious inquiries into UAPs that deserve our focus.

What’s next for Anna Paulina Luna? Will she lead a congressional inquiry into the connections between ancient texts and modern technology?
