U.S. Treasury Proposes New Crypto Regulations to Enhance Consumer Protection

By Airyan Fawking — 2025-08-13 05:53 UTC

On October 10, 2023, the U.S. Treasury Department announced a set of proposed regulations aimed at enhancing consumer protection in the cryptocurrency market. The new rules focus on increasing transparency and accountability among digital asset service providers.

Proposed Regulations Target Financial Crimes

The proposed regulations seek to address concerns over the potential use of cryptocurrencies in financial crimes, including money laundering and fraud. The Treasury emphasized the need for a robust regulatory framework to protect consumers and ensure the integrity of the financial system.

Key Features of the Proposal

  • Increased reporting requirements for digital asset transactions.
  • Mandatory registration for cryptocurrency exchanges and wallet providers.
  • Enhanced customer due diligence measures.
“These proposals are designed to safeguard consumers and promote responsible innovation in the cryptocurrency space,” said Treasury Secretary Janet Yellen.

Context and Industry Response

The announcement comes amid growing scrutiny of the cryptocurrency sector, which has faced criticism for its lack of regulation and potential risks to investors. Industry leaders have expressed mixed reactions, with some welcoming the clarity while others argue that excessive regulation could stifle innovation.

Looking Ahead

Public comments on the proposed regulations will be accepted for 60 days, after which the Treasury will review feedback before finalizing the rules. The outcome of this process could significantly shape the future of cryptocurrency regulation in the United States.
