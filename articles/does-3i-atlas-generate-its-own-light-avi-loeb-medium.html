Does 3I/ATLAS Generate Its Own Light?

The Hubble Space Telescope has provided the best image of the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS on July 21, 2025, revealing a glow of light likely from a coma ahead of its motion towards the Sun. No cometary tail has been observed, leading researchers to suggest that this glow results from dust evaporation on the Sun-facing side of 3I/ATLAS.

This finding raises questions about the nature of 3I/ATLAS. Avi Loeb, a researcher at Harvard, has proposed that 3I/ATLAS may generate its own light rather than simply reflecting sunlight. This hypothesis challenges previous estimates of its size, suggesting it could be significantly smaller than the 20-kilometer diameter inferred from the reflection model.

Key facts

  • 3I/ATLAS was imaged by the Hubble Space Telescope on July 21, 2025.
  • The object is expected to pass within 28.96 million kilometers of Mars on October 3, 2025.
  • Avi Loeb is the director of the Institute for Theory and Computation at Harvard University.
  • 3I/ATLAS shows a steep brightness profile with a projected power-law slope of -4.
  • The luminosity of 3I/ATLAS is calculated to be around 10 gigawatts.

Timeline

On July 21, 2025, the Hubble Space Telescope captured an image of 3I/ATLAS, revealing a glow consistent with dust evaporation. Following this, Avi Loeb and his colleague Eric Keto analyzed the brightness profile and proposed that the object may generate its own light.

Loeb calculated that if 3I/ATLAS produces its own light, it could be much smaller than previously thought, with a potential diameter under 100 meters. This conclusion contrasts with earlier models suggesting a size of up to 20 kilometers based on sunlight reflection.

Loeb has called for observations of 3I/ATLAS using the HiRISE camera on the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter during its close approach to Mars in October 2025.

Official statements

Avi Loeb stated, "The nucleus dominates the observed light. This must hold irrespective of the origin of the light."

Context

3I/ATLAS is one of several interstellar objects observed in recent years. Previous objects, such as 1I/`Oumuamua and 2I/Borisov, have also prompted discussions regarding their origins and characteristics. The potential for 3I/ATLAS to be a technological object has emerged due to its unusual brightness profile and trajectory.

What’s next

Researchers plan to gather more data on 3I/ATLAS during its close approach to Mars in October 2025, which could provide further insights into its nature and characteristics.
