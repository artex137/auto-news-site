Independent Journalist Molly White Knows How to Follow the Memecoin

In an era where misinformation runs rampant and the truth often takes a back seat to sensationalism, Molly White emerges as a beacon of integrity in the murky waters of cryptocurrency journalism. This independent journalist and software engineer has dedicated herself to unraveling the tangled web of financial dealings that characterize the crypto industry. With her astute observations and sharp critiques, White is not just following the money—she’s exposing the dark underbelly of a multi-billion dollar industry that is keen on manipulating political landscapes.

White's investigative platform, Follow the Crypto, has tracked cryptocurrency spending that rivals the financial clout of the energy sector and big pharma during the 2024 election cycle. That's right, folks! While you were busy scrolling through social media, the crypto industry was pouring money into political campaigns, and White was there to document every shocking detail. According to her findings, this financial influx was not merely a coincidence; it was a calculated strategy to influence Congress and promote crypto-friendly legislation.

“When I look back through my writing over that year, I can see my own thought process developing,” White explained. “At first, I wondered why they were spending so much on candidates like Katie Porter and Cori Bush, who rarely discussed crypto. It dawned on me that this was a show of force—a marketing strategy to intimidate Congresspeople into aligning with their agenda.”

With a keen eye for detail, White has been chronicling the crypto industry’s political spending, revealing that it was not exclusively aimed at supporting Republicans, as many might assume. Instead, her research unveiled a more complex picture: an extensive outreach to Democrats as well. As the presidential election approached, the crypto industry's last-minute push to court Vice President Kamala Harris is a prime example of their strategy, showcasing their ambition to infiltrate both sides of the political aisle.

Furthermore, White’s work shines a light on the legislative successes that have emerged from this financial onslaught. Three crypto-friendly bills recently passed the House, and one even made it through the Senate to be signed into law. The Senate has confirmed numerous pro-crypto nominees, indicating that the industry’s financial investments have indeed paid off. But the real question remains: at what cost?

“I think there has been a considerable payoff for the industry,” White stated, highlighting the alarming trend of political lobbying and financial entanglements that could compromise regulatory integrity. “There are reports of White House employees holding substantial crypto investments, which raises ethical questions about their ability to regulate the industry.”

What’s more, White is not content to merely report on these developments. She’s actively working to enhance her platform by incorporating lobbying data into her analysis, emphasizing the importance of transparency in political contributions and investments. “I’m hoping to follow the extent to which individual Congresspeople are invested in the industry,” she noted, pointing to the potential conflicts of interest that could arise when lawmakers have a financial stake in the very industry they are meant to oversee.

But let’s not forget the broader implications of White’s work. Her newsletter, Citation Needed, is not just a repository of information; it’s a call to action for those who care about the truth. With around 29,000 subscribers and a pay-what-you-want model, she is challenging the norms of traditional journalism by providing accessible, well-researched content that empowers readers to navigate the treacherous waters of the crypto landscape.

“I created that tool to provide a lens into the spending happening without people having to wade through messy filings,” White explained, emphasizing her commitment to making complex information digestible for the average citizen.

In a world where algorithms dictate what we read and how we engage with information, White advocates for a return to the roots of journalism. She champions the use of RSS feeds for content discovery and encourages readers to seek out diverse perspectives rather than relying on curated content from tech giants. “It’s like the old days when people shared interesting things without the algorithmic black box,” she said, lamenting the loss of genuine discovery in the digital age.

As a critic of the tech industry, White's skepticism extends to platforms like Substack, which she left in favor of Ghost, a move motivated by both ethical concerns and financial considerations. “As my newsletter grew, a large amount of money was going to Substack, and that didn’t sit well with me,” she admitted, showcasing her dedication to maintaining her independence.

In conclusion, Molly White is not just another journalist; she is a trailblazer in the fight for transparency and accountability in the cryptocurrency industry. Her work serves as a reminder that following the money is not only a journalistic mantra but a civic duty. As the crypto landscape continues to evolve, one thing is certain: White will be there, shining a light on the shadows and holding the powerful accountable.
