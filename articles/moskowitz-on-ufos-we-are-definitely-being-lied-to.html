Moskowitz On UFOs: 'We Are Definitely Being Lied To'

During a House Oversight Committee hearing on unidentified anomalous phenomena (UAPs) on October 25, 2023, Rep. Jared Moskowitz (D-FL) stated that credible witnesses have come forward to discuss their experiences with UAPs. Moskowitz emphasized that these witnesses, including former military personnel, possess impeccable records.

This statement highlights ongoing concerns regarding transparency surrounding UAPs. Moskowitz's remarks suggest a growing belief among lawmakers that official narratives may not fully represent the truth about these phenomena. The hearing is part of a broader effort by Congress to investigate UAPs and their potential implications for national security.

Key facts

  • Date of hearing: October 25, 2023
  • Location: House Oversight Committee, Washington, D.C.
  • Witnesses: Former military personnel with strong credentials
  • Rep. Jared Moskowitz: Democrat from Florida

Timeline

On October 25, 2023, Rep. Moskowitz addressed witnesses at the House Oversight Committee hearing, focusing on UAPs. He described the witnesses as highly credible and expressed concerns about the integrity of official accounts relating to UAPs.

The hearing is part of ongoing congressional efforts to investigate UAPs, with an emphasis on gathering testimonies from credible sources.

Official statements

“The narrative has changed,” Moskowitz said, referring to the evolving discourse surrounding UAPs.
“We are definitely being lied to,” he added, contrasting the whistleblowers' reputations with the official accounts.

Context

UAPs have gained increasing attention in recent years, with multiple reports of sightings and encounters from military personnel. The U.S. government has initiated investigations to better understand these phenomena, reflecting a trend toward greater transparency and public interest.

What’s next

Congress is expected to continue its investigations into UAPs, with further hearings and testimonies planned in the coming months. The focus will remain on gathering credible information from witnesses to clarify the government's position on UAPs.
