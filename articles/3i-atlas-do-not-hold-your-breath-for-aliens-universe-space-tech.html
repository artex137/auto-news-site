3I/ATLAS: Do Not Hold Your Breath for Aliens - Universe Space Tech

The comet 3I/ATLAS (C/2025 N1), discovered on July 1, 2025, by the ATLAS robotic telescope system, has gained significant attention in the field of astronomy. The object, which is believed to have originated from interstellar space, was detected beyond Jupiter's orbit. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with the comet.

This discovery is important as it marks the third known interstellar object to enter the Solar System. It provides scientists with unique insights into the composition and characteristics of celestial bodies from outside our solar system. Astronomers are currently conducting extensive observations of 3I/ATLAS to learn more about its properties and origin.

Key facts

  • Discovered on July 1, 2025, by the ATLAS telescope.
  • Located beyond Jupiter's orbit.
  • Estimated speed upon entering the Solar System: 58 km/s.
  • Eccentricity of the orbit: 6.141.
  • Estimated age: between 7.6 and 13 billion years.
  • Diameter of the comet's nucleus: between 0.32 and 5.6 km.
  • Normal cometary activity observed, including formation of a coma and tail.

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, the ATLAS robotic telescope system discovered 3I/ATLAS. Initial observations indicated that it was a comet originating from the Oort cloud, although no cometary activity was detected at that time. Following its discovery, astronomers redirected powerful instruments to study the object, confirming its nature as a comet.

Throughout July, scientists observed that 3I/ATLAS exhibited normal cometary activity, including a coma and tail formation. Spectroscopic studies revealed the comet's composition, which includes water, hydroxyl ions, carbon dioxide, and carbon monoxide, while larger dust particles were also detected.

Official statements

“The appearance of the third interstellar object was eagerly awaited. The rapid advancement in astronomical technology has allowed us to see things in the sky that previously went unnoticed,” said a spokesperson from the astronomical community.

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third interstellar object recorded in human history, following the discovery of the asteroid Oumuamua in 2017 and the comet Borisov in 2019. The increased detection of interstellar objects has been attributed to advancements in astronomical technology, including large survey telescopes and space observatories.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it travels through the Solar System. Further observations and studies are planned to refine estimates of its size and to better understand its composition and origin.
