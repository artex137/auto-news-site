3I/ATLAS: Do Not Hold Your Breath for Aliens, Says Universe Space Tech

The interstellar comet 3I/ATLAS (C/2025 N1) has been making waves in the scientific community since its discovery by the ATLAS robotic telescope system on July 1, 2025. The comet, which is believed to have originated from another star, has raised speculation about its nature, with some suggesting it could be an alien probe. However, experts emphasize that its characteristics align with those of a typical comet.

This discovery is significant as it marks the third known interstellar object, following the asteroid Oumuamua and the comet Borisov. Authorities have mobilized powerful astronomical instruments to study 3I/ATLAS, which is providing valuable insights into interstellar objects, their origins, and their compositions.

Key facts

  • Discovery Date: July 1, 2025
  • Location: Beyond Jupiter’s orbit
  • Speed: 58 km/s upon entering the Solar System
  • Eccentricity: 6.141
  • Estimated Age: Between 7.6 and 13 billion years
  • Diameter of Nucleus: Estimated between 0.32 and 5.6 km
  • Cometary Activity: Exhibits normal activity, including a coma and tail

Timeline

On July 1, 2025, the ATLAS robotic telescope system discovered 3I/ATLAS, which showed rapid movement against the backdrop of stars. Initial observations indicated no cometary activity, but further studies confirmed its identity as a comet from interstellar space.

Throughout July 2025, astronomers observed the comet and confirmed its cometary activity, detecting water, hydroxyl ions, carbon dioxide, and carbon monoxide. They also noted that it was ejecting dust, with particles approximately 1 micron in diameter.

On July 16, 2025, Avi Loeb, a Harvard astrophysics professor, suggested that 3I/ATLAS could be an alien probe, prompting a wave of speculation and debate among scientists and the public.

Official statements

“The appearance of 3I/ATLAS is an exciting development in our understanding of interstellar objects,” said a spokesperson from the astronomical community. “While speculation about its nature is common, the evidence supports its classification as a comet.”

Context

3I/ATLAS is the third macroscopic interstellar object identified to date, following Oumuamua in 2017 and Borisov in 2019. The increasing number of detected interstellar objects highlights advancements in astronomical technology and observation capabilities over recent decades.

Prior to these discoveries, interstellar visitors had not been recorded, but advancements in survey telescopes and data sharing among astronomers have improved detection rates.

What’s next

Scientists will continue to monitor 3I/ATLAS as it travels through the Solar System, conducting further spectroscopic studies to learn more about its composition and behavior. There are no current plans for missions to intercept or study the comet up close.
