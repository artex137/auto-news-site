UFO Drummer Opens Up on 'Vitriol' Between Michael and Rudolf Schenker

UFO drummer Vinnie Moore has discussed the longstanding tensions between band members Michael and Rudolf Schenker in a recent interview. He described their relationship as filled with "vitriol" and characterized both musicians as an "interesting lot." The conversation took place nine days ago, shedding light on internal conflicts that have affected the band's dynamics over the years.

This revelation matters as it highlights the complexities within UFO, a band that has had a significant impact on the rock music scene. The internal struggles may have contributed to their challenges in achieving greater success in the United States, as Moore noted in his interview.

Key facts

  • Vinnie Moore is the current drummer for UFO.
  • The interview took place nine days ago.
  • Michael Schenker and Rudolf Schenker are founding members of UFO.
  • Phil Mogg, the frontman, stated that UFO has come to a conclusion as a band.
  • UFO's founding bassist, Pete Way, passed away at the age of 69 five years ago.

Timeline

In recent months, UFO has faced various changes and challenges. Eight months ago, Michael Schenker revealed his reasons for leaving the band, stating, "I had my own vision." A year ago, Vinnie Moore shared insights about his current musical setup. Additionally, the band's frontman, Phil Mogg, announced a year ago that he believed UFO had reached a conclusion.

Three years ago, a performance was marred by audience hostility, with cans reportedly being thrown at the band. This incident, along with internal conflicts, may have influenced the band’s trajectory.

Official statements

"I can never decide, my board is never final," Vinnie Moore said, referencing his ongoing musical journey.
"I think it's come to a conclusion," Phil Mogg remarked about the future of UFO.

Context

UFO has faced various internal and external challenges throughout its history. The band's founding members have had differing visions, which has led to conflicts and changes in lineup over the years. The passing of bassist Pete Way also marked a significant loss for the group.

Despite their influence and contributions to rock music, UFO has struggled to maintain a consistent presence in the competitive U.S. market, as noted by Moore in his recent interview.

What’s next

As of now, there are no disclosed plans for future projects or reunions involving UFO. The band appears to be at a standstill following Mogg's statement about its conclusion.
