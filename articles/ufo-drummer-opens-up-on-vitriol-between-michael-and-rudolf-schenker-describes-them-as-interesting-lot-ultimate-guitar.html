UFO Drummer Opens Up on 'Vitriol' Between Michael and Rudolf Schenker

UFO drummer Vinnie Moore has discussed the ongoing tensions between band members Michael and Rudolf Schenker, describing them as an "interesting lot." Moore shared his insights recently, highlighting the discord that has persisted within the band. No injuries or fatalities have been reported in connection with this situation.

This matter is significant as it sheds light on the internal dynamics of the band UFO, which has had a notable influence on the rock music scene. The discussion may provide fans and industry observers with a clearer understanding of the relationships that have shaped the band's history.

Key facts

  • Vinnie Moore is the drummer for UFO.
  • Michael and Rudolf Schenker are founding members of the band.
  • Moore described the relationship between the Schenker brothers as filled with "vitriol."
  • UFO has faced challenges in gaining wider recognition in the United States.
  • UFO's frontman, Phil Mogg, has indicated that the band has reached a conclusion.

Timeline

In recent discussions, Vinnie Moore has reflected on the band's past and the reasons they struggled to achieve greater success in the U.S. He noted the internal conflicts that have existed for years.

Michael Schenker revealed eight months ago that he left UFO to pursue his own vision, indicating a long-standing rift within the group.

Phil Mogg announced a year ago that he believes UFO has come to a conclusion, suggesting the end of the band's journey.

Official statements

“We were so close,” Vinnie Moore said, reflecting on the band's potential and its challenges.
“I had my own vision,” Michael Schenker stated regarding his departure from the band.
“I think it’s come to a conclusion,” Phil Mogg revealed about the band's future.

Context

UFO has been a significant player in the rock genre since its formation, but it has faced various internal struggles over the years. The tensions between band members, particularly the Schenker brothers, have been well documented, affecting the group’s cohesion and public perception.

Prior incidents of discord include a "horrible" pre-breakup gig where the audience reportedly threw cans at the band, illustrating the challenges they faced in their performances.

What’s next

As the band members continue to reflect on their past, it remains unclear what the future holds for UFO. Investigations into their internal dynamics may provide further insights, but no official announcements have been made regarding future projects or reunions.
