UFO Drummer Opens Up on 'Vitriol' Between Michael and Rudolf Schenker, Describes Them as 'Interesting Lot'

UFO drummer Vinnie Moore has discussed the ongoing tensions between band members Michael Schenker and Rudolf Schenker, describing them as an "interesting lot." This revelation comes as part of a broader conversation about the band's history and dynamics. The comments were made in a recent interview, although the specific date and location of the interview have not been disclosed.

This discussion is significant as it sheds light on the internal conflicts that have affected UFO's trajectory over the years. According to Moore, the relationship between the Schenker brothers has been marked by "vitriol," indicating a longstanding rift that may have contributed to the band's struggles in achieving greater success in the United States.

Key facts

  • Vinnie Moore is the current drummer for UFO.
  • Michael Schenker and Rudolf Schenker are founding members of the band.
  • Moore described the Schenker brothers as an "interesting lot" in a recent interview.
  • The band has faced challenges in the U.S. market, as noted by Moore.
  • UFO has had a storied history, with various line-up changes over the years.

Timeline

UFO has experienced various changes in its lineup and management since its formation. The band's dynamics have often been influenced by the relationships between its members. In the past, Michael Schenker left UFO to pursue his own vision, which has been a source of tension within the band.

Over the years, there have been several incidents highlighting the conflicts among band members, including a particularly "horrible" gig where the audience reacted negatively, throwing cans at the band. These events have contributed to the perception of discord within UFO.

Official statements

"I had my own vision," said Michael Schenker, explaining his departure from the band.

"I think it's come to a conclusion," said frontman Phil Mogg, reflecting on the band's status.

Context

UFO has been a prominent name in the rock music scene since the 1970s. The band has undergone several lineup changes, with tensions between members often making headlines. The death of founding bassist Pete Way at the age of 69 marked a significant loss for the group and its fans.

Despite their influential sound and legacy, UFO has struggled to maintain a consistent presence in the U.S. market, which has been a point of frustration for its members.

What’s next

As the band reflects on its past, there are no announced plans for future projects or reunions. The ongoing discussions among members may influence any potential directions UFO might take moving forward.
