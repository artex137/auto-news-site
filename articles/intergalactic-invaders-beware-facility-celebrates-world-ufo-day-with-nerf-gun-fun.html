Intergalactic invaders beware: Facility celebrates World UFO Day with Nerf gun fun

On July 2, The Highlands skilled nursing and rehabilitation facility in Fitchburg, MA, celebrated World UFO Day with a themed event featuring games and Nerf gun activities. The event did not report any injuries or fatalities.

This celebration marks the anniversary of an alleged UFO landing in Roswell, NM, in 1947, which has fueled curiosity and conspiracy theories across the country. According to Activities Director Kristi Mendoza, the event provided a unique opportunity for residents and staff to engage in fun, interactive activities that fostered community spirit.

Key facts

  • Date of event: July 2
  • Location: The Highlands skilled nursing and rehabilitation, Fitchburg, MA
  • Participants: More than 20 residents and staff members
  • Activities included: Tossing aliens, reeling them in, and Nerf gun battles

Timeline

On July 2, The Highlands hosted its first UFO-themed event, which included various alien-themed games. The planning involved collaboration among staff from activities, nursing, dietary, and maintenance departments. Mendoza utilized online resources for inspiration and materials.

The event featured activities like tossing aliens through Hula Hoops and shooting them with Nerf guns, which residents particularly enjoyed. Mendoza noted that the event created a joyful atmosphere, allowing participants to embrace their inner child.

Official statements

“Each month when calendar planning, I look for unique days to celebrate as a starting point for inspiration,” said Activities Director Kristi Mendoza. “It was an afternoon full of laughter and fun that comes with letting go and enjoying time to be a little silly embracing the inner child in us all.”

Context

World UFO Day has become synonymous with curiosity about extraterrestrial life since the Roswell incident in 1947. Facilities like The Highlands are increasingly incorporating themed events into their activities to enhance resident engagement and well-being.

What’s next

The Highlands plans to continue hosting creative events, including a potential return of the UFO theme next year. Mendoza expressed enthusiasm for future activities that promote fun and interaction among residents and staff.
