Comet 3I/ATLAS changes course as it approaches Earth

The interstellar object known as 3I/ATLAS has displayed unusual changes in shape and colour as it nears Earth, according to astronomers in Austria. Observations made on September 7 revealed that the comet has shifted from a reddish hue to a greenish tone, a phenomenon that current comet models do not explain.

This discovery is significant as it challenges existing theories about comet behaviour. Astronomers have begun detailed observations, noting rapid changes in the light surrounding 3I/ATLAS. The comet is expected to come within 168 million kilometres of Earth on December 19, 2025.

Key facts

  • Object: 3I/ATLAS
  • Observation date: September 7, 2023
  • Expected closest approach to Earth: December 19, 2025
  • Distance at closest approach: 168 million kilometres
  • Cyanide emission rate: 20 grams per second
  • Nickel emission rate: 5 grams per second
  • Primary observing location: Namibia

Timeline

On August 25, the Very Large Telescope observed that 3I/ATLAS was emitting significant amounts of cyanide. By August 28, the comet's tail began to brighten, indicating changes in its physical state. Detailed imaging on September 7 captured the comet's transition to a greenish hue.

Michael Jäger, an astronomer involved in the observations, explained that the changes in brightness were linked to the release of volatile materials as the comet approached the Sun.

Official statements

"During the full moon eclipse, we captured detailed images of Comet 3I/ATLAS from the dark skies of Namibia," said Michael Jäger. "By combining multiple wavelengths of light, we were able to clearly see the coma of the comet."
"The data suggests a significant increase in cyanide production, which is unusual for comets," stated Avi Loeb, a physicist at Harvard. "This could hint at industrial processes rather than natural cometary behaviour."

Context

This is not the first interstellar object observed. Previous objects include 1I/ʻOumuamua and 2I/Borisov, which did not exhibit similar volatile characteristics. The unique traits of 3I/ATLAS have prompted scientists to reconsider their understanding of interstellar bodies.

What’s next

Further investigations will continue as the comet approaches the Sun. Researchers aim to analyze the changes in composition and behaviour of 3I/ATLAS to gain insights into its origins and nature.
