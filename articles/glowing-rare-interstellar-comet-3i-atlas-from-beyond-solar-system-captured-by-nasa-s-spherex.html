Glowing rare interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS from beyond Solar System captured by NASA’s SPHEREx

NASA’s SPHEREx mission has successfully captured images of the rare interstellar Comet 3I/ATLAS, which is currently passing through our Solar System. The comet was detected as it approached Earth, showcasing its unique glow and characteristics.

This event is significant as it provides scientists with valuable data about the composition of interstellar objects. NASA officials have stated that studying Comet 3I/ATLAS can help enhance our understanding of the formation of solar systems and the materials present in them.

Key facts

  • Comet 3I/ATLAS is an interstellar object.
  • NASA’s SPHEREx mission captured images of the comet.
  • The comet is currently passing through the Solar System.
  • Data from the mission will aid in understanding solar system formation.
  • Details about the comet's exact trajectory and timing are not disclosed.

Timeline

Comet 3I/ATLAS was first identified as it entered the Solar System. NASA's SPHEREx mission, which launched to explore the cosmos, has been observing the comet to gather data. The mission aims to analyze the light from the comet to better understand its composition and origin.

Official statements

“Studying Comet 3I/ATLAS offers a unique opportunity to learn about the building blocks of our solar system,” said a NASA spokesperson.

Context

Interstellar comets are rare, with only a few identified since the first detection. Each provides crucial insights into the materials that exist beyond our Solar System, helping to shape theories about the formation of planetary systems.

What’s next

NASA plans to continue monitoring Comet 3I/ATLAS as it moves through the Solar System, with further analysis expected to yield more insights into its properties and origins.
